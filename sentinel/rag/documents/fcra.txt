FAIR CREDIT REPORTING ACT (FCRA)
15 U.S.C. §§ 1681 et seq. — Relevant Provisions for AI/ML Financial Matching

SECTION 601: PURPOSE

The purpose of the FCRA is to require that consumer reporting agencies adopt reasonable
procedures for meeting the needs of commerce for consumer credit, personnel, insurance,
and other information in a manner which is fair and equitable to the consumer, with regard
to the confidentiality, accuracy, relevancy, and proper utilization of such information.

SECTION 604: PERMISSIBLE PURPOSES OF CONSUMER REPORTS

A consumer reporting agency may furnish a consumer report under the following circumstances:
(a) In accordance with the written instructions of the consumer
(b) To a person which it has reason to believe intends to use the information in connection
    with a credit transaction involving the consumer
(c) For employment purposes
(d) In connection with the underwriting of insurance
(e) In connection with a legitimate business need in connection with a consumer-initiated
    business transaction

AI SYSTEMS AND FCRA APPLICABILITY

When an AI/ML system uses consumer data to make matching recommendations that affect
access to financial services or advisors, FCRA principles apply:

1. Accuracy: The AI system must produce accurate results. Models must be regularly
   validated to ensure prediction accuracy.

2. Relevancy: Input features used by the model must be relevant to the legitimate
   purpose of the matching decision.

3. Adverse Action Requirements: When a consumer is denied a service or receives a
   less favorable outcome based on model output, the consumer must receive:
   - Written notice of the adverse action
   - The name and contact information of the model/agency
   - A statement that the decision was based on model output
   - The consumer's right to request a free copy of their report/model inputs
   - The consumer's right to dispute inaccurate information

SECTION 615: REQUIREMENTS ON USERS OF CONSUMER REPORTS

Any person who takes any adverse action with respect to any consumer that is based
in whole or in part on any information contained in a consumer report shall:
(1) Provide oral, written, or electronic notice of the adverse action to the consumer
(2) Provide to the consumer the name, address, and telephone number of the consumer
    reporting agency that furnished the report
(3) Provide the consumer with a written or electronic disclosure of:
    (A) a statement that the consumer reporting agency did not make the decision to
        take the adverse action and is unable to provide the consumer the specific
        reasons why the adverse action was taken
    (B) the consumer's right to obtain, under section 612, a free copy of a consumer
        report on the consumer from the consumer reporting agency
    (C) the consumer's right to dispute, under section 611, with a consumer reporting
        agency the accuracy or completeness of any information in a consumer report

AI-SPECIFIC ADVERSE ACTION REQUIREMENTS

For AI/ML-driven adverse actions:
- The explanation must identify the principal reasons (typically top 3-4 SHAP features)
- Reasons must be specific enough to allow the consumer to take remedial action
- Generic reasons such as "model score too low" are insufficient
- The explanation should be in plain language understandable to a lay person

SECTION 616: CIVIL LIABILITY FOR WILLFUL NONCOMPLIANCE

Any person who willfully fails to comply with any requirement imposed under this title
with respect to any consumer is liable to that consumer for:
(1) any actual damages sustained by the consumer as a result of the failure
(2) such amount of punitive damages as the court may allow
(3) in the case of any successful action to enforce any liability, the costs of the
    action together with reasonable attorney's fees
