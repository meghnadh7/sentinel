REGULATION B — EQUAL CREDIT OPPORTUNITY ACT
12 CFR Part 202 — Relevant Provisions for AI/ML Financial Services

PURPOSE

Regulation B prohibits creditors from discriminating against credit applicants on the
basis of race, color, religion, national origin, sex, marital status, age (provided
the applicant has the capacity to contract), because all or part of the applicant's
income derives from any public assistance program, or because the applicant has in good
faith exercised any right under the Consumer Credit Protection Act.

SECTION 202.4: GENERAL RULES

(a) Discrimination. A creditor shall not discriminate against an applicant on a prohibited
    basis regarding any aspect of a credit transaction.

(b) Discouragement. A creditor shall not make any oral or written statement, in advertising
    or otherwise, to applicants or prospective applicants that would discourage on a
    prohibited basis a reasonable person from making or pursuing an application.

SECTION 202.9: NOTIFICATIONS

(a) Notification of action taken, ECOA notice, and statement of specific reasons.

(1) A creditor shall notify an applicant of action taken within:
    (i) 30 days after receiving a completed application concerning the creditor's
        approval of, counteroffer to, or adverse action on the application
    (ii) 30 days after taking adverse action on an incomplete application

(2) Content of notification when adverse action is taken. A notification given to an
    applicant when adverse action is taken shall be in writing and shall contain:
    (i) A statement of the action taken
    (ii) The name and address of the creditor
    (iii) A statement of the provisions of section 701(a) of the Act
    (iv) The name and address of the Federal agency that administers compliance with
         respect to the creditor
    (v) Either:
        (A) A statement of specific reasons for the action taken, or
        (B) A disclosure of the applicant's right to a statement of specific reasons

AI/ML MODEL SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS UNDER REGULATION B

For adverse action taken based on AI/ML model outputs:

1. Specific Reasons Requirement: Financial institutions must be able to provide specific
   reasons for adverse actions taken by AI/ML models. "Model decision" alone is not
   sufficient — the institution must be able to articulate the principal factors driving
   the model's output.

2. CFPB Guidance on AI Adverse Action (2023):
   - Institutions may use SHAP values or similar explainability methods to identify
     the principal reasons for an adverse action
   - The reasons provided must be accurate reflections of the model's actual decision
     factors, not post-hoc rationalizations
   - Institutions must validate that their explainability methodology actually explains
     the model's behavior

3. Protected Characteristics:
   Models must not use the following as input features (directly or as proxies):
   - Race, color, religion, national origin
   - Sex or gender
   - Marital status
   - Age (except as legally permitted)
   - Receipt of income from public assistance programs

4. Proxy Discrimination:
   Even features that appear neutral may constitute illegal discrimination if they
   serve as proxies for protected characteristics. Regular disparate impact testing
   is required.

SECTION 202.11: RELATION TO STATE LAW

This regulation does not annul, alter, or affect, or exempt any person subject to the
provisions of this regulation from complying with, the laws of any State with respect
to credit discrimination, except to the extent that those laws are inconsistent with
any provision of the Equal Credit Opportunity Act or this regulation, and then only to
the extent of the inconsistency.

DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS FOR AI SYSTEMS

Financial institutions using AI/ML for credit-related decisions must document:
- Training data composition and known demographic distributions
- Feature selection rationale (demonstrating relevance to legitimate credit factors)
- Regular disparate impact testing results across all protected classes
- Explainability methodology and validation that it accurately reflects model behavior
- Process for providing adverse action notices based on model outputs
- Consumer complaint handling for AI-driven decisions
