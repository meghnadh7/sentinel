NIST AI RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK (AI RMF 1.0)
National Institute of Standards and Technology — January 2023

INTRODUCTION

The NIST AI RMF is intended to offer a resource to the broad AI community — including
AI developers, users, evaluators, and others — for improving the ability to incorporate
trustworthiness considerations into the design, development, use, and evaluation of AI
products, services, and systems.

THE FOUR CORE FUNCTIONS

GOVERN — Cultivate and Implement AI Risk Management Culture

GOVERN 1: POLICIES, PROCESSES, PROCEDURES, AND PRACTICES

GOVERN 1.1: Organizational policies are in place to address AI risks and benefits.
GOVERN 1.2: Mechanisms are in place to inventory AI systems.
GOVERN 1.3: Organizational teams are committed to a culture that considers and communicates
            AI risk and creates and maintains AI documentation.
GOVERN 1.4: Organizational teams are committed to and model the imperative of AI safety.
GOVERN 1.5: Organizational policies and practices are in place to define and differentiate
            roles for those involved in the various activities of the AI lifecycle.
GOVERN 1.6: Policies and procedures are in place to address AI risks and benefits arising
            from third-party software and data and other supply chain issues.
GOVERN 1.7: Processes and procedures are in place for decommissioning and phasing out
            AI systems safely and in a manner that does not increase risks or decrease
            the organization's trustworthiness.

GOVERN 2: ACCOUNTABILITY

GOVERN 2.1: Roles and responsibilities and lines of communication related to mapping,
            measuring, and managing AI risks are documented and are clear to individuals
            and teams throughout the organization.
GOVERN 2.2: The organization's personnel and partners receive AI risk management training
            to enable them to perform their duties and responsibilities consistent with
            related policies, procedures, and agreements.

GOVERN 3: TEAM CULTURE

GOVERN 3.1: Decision-making related to mapping, measuring, and managing AI risks includes
            input from diverse teams with varied perspectives and relevant domain expertise.
GOVERN 3.2: Teams are committed to a culture of equality and inclusion.

GOVERN 4: ORGANIZATIONAL TEAMS

GOVERN 4.1: Organizational policies and practices are in place to foster a critical
            thinking and safety-first mindset in the design, development, deployment,
            and uses of AI systems to minimize potential negative impacts.
GOVERN 4.2: Organizational teams document the risks and potential impacts of AI technologies.

GOVERN 5: ORGANIZATIONAL RISK POLICIES

GOVERN 5.1: Organizational policies and practices are in place to collect, consider, and
            prioritize the development of AI systems that are aligned with human values.
GOVERN 5.2: Organizational teams are committed to and implement processes enabling regular
            engagement of diverse communities and stakeholders.

GOVERN 6: POLICIES AND PROCESSES FOR AI RISK

GOVERN 6.1: Policies and procedures are in place to address risks and benefits of
            organizational AI deployment.
GOVERN 6.2: Contingency processes are in place to handle failures or incidents in AI systems.

MAP — Categorize and Prioritize AI Risks

MAP 1: CONTEXT IS ESTABLISHED

MAP 1.1: Context is established for the AI risk assessment, including the intended and
         unintended uses of AI systems.
MAP 1.2: Interdisciplinary AI actors, users, and other stakeholders are identified and
         their roles, responsibilities, and potential impacts are documented.
MAP 1.3: The business value or context in which the AI system will be used is identified.
MAP 1.4: The team that developed or deployed the AI system is identified.
MAP 1.5: Organizational risk tolerance is determined and documented.
MAP 1.6: Risks or other undesirable impacts of the AI system to individuals, groups,
         communities, organizations, and society are identified.

MAP 2: SCIENTIFIC UNDERSTANDING OF IMPACTS IS ENHANCED

MAP 2.1: Scientific findings related to AI risk over the technology's lifecycle are
         reviewed and incorporated by the team.
MAP 2.2: Scientific understanding of the AI lifecycle and its risks informs organizational
         policies, processes, and practices.
MAP 2.3: AI system risks and benefits attributable to third-party software, data,
         algorithms, models, or human resources are mapped and communicated.

MAP 3: AI RISKS AND BENEFITS ARE MAPPED FOR ORGANIZATIONAL USE

MAP 3.1: Potential benefits of intended AI system deployment are examined and documented.
MAP 3.2: AI system functionality and behavior are evaluated to determine whether they meet
         intended goals and whether unintended functionality or behaviors are present.
MAP 3.3: AI system risks and benefits attributable to bias, fairness, and representativeness
         are mapped.
MAP 3.4: AI system risks and benefits attributable to explainability and interpretability
         are mapped.
MAP 3.5: AI system risks and benefits attributable to reliability and safety are mapped.

MAP 4: RISKS ARE PRIORITIZED BASED ON IMPACT AND LIKELIHOOD

MAP 4.1: Risks are prioritized based on their impacts to individuals and communities,
         as well as the organization and broader society.
MAP 4.2: Risk levels are documented.

MAP 5: IDENTIFIED AI RISKS ARE MAPPED TO ORGANIZATIONAL PRIORITIES

MAP 5.1: Likelihood of the AI risk being realized is estimated.
MAP 5.2: Practices and personnel for supporting AI risk identification are in place.
MAP 5.3: Interdisciplinary AI actors, users, and other stakeholders are identified and
         their roles and responsibilities regarding AI risk are documented.

MEASURE — Analyze and Assess AI Risks

MEASURE 1: APPROACHES ARE IDENTIFIED AND SELECTED

MEASURE 1.1: Approaches and methods for measuring the identified AI risks are identified
             and prioritized, with the goal of making them actionable.
MEASURE 1.2: Assessments and plans are in place to regularly test, calibrate, and track
             AI performance metrics and outcomes.
MEASURE 1.3: Internal experts who did not serve as front-line developers are involved
             in assessing AI risks.

MEASURE 2: AI SYSTEMS ARE EVALUATED FOR TRUSTWORTHY CHARACTERISTICS

MEASURE 2.1: Test sets, metrics, and details about the tools used during test and evaluation,
             validation, and verification are documented.
MEASURE 2.2: Evaluations involving human subjects are protected and conducted in accordance
             with applicable ethics guidelines.
MEASURE 2.3: AI system performance or assurance criteria are measured qualitatively or
             quantitatively and documented.
MEASURE 2.4: The functionality and behavior of the AI system and its components — as
             identified in the MAP function — are monitored when in production.
MEASURE 2.5: The AI system to be deployed undergoes routine testing and validation.
MEASURE 2.6: The risks or other potential impacts of the AI system are examined across
             the AI lifecycle.
MEASURE 2.7: AI system security and resilience — such as adversarial testing — are
             evaluated and documented.
MEASURE 2.8: Risks associated with transparency and accountability are examined and documented.
MEASURE 2.9: Privacy risk of the AI system — as identified in the MAP function — is
             examined and documented.
MEASURE 2.10: Privacy risk of the AI system — as identified in the MAP function — is
              examined and documented.
MEASURE 2.11: Fairness and bias — as identified in the MAP function — is evaluated for
              impacts on individuals, groups, and the broader society.
MEASURE 2.12: Environmental impact and sustainability of AI model development and operation
              is measured.
MEASURE 2.13: Effectiveness of the employed TEVV metrics and processes in the context of
              the AI lifecycle stage is evaluated and documented.

MEASURE 3: AI RISKS ARE TRACKED OVER TIME

MEASURE 3.1: Approaches, personnel, and documentation are in place to regularly identify
             and track existing, unanticipated, and emergent AI risks.
MEASURE 3.2: Risk tracking approaches are considered for settings where AI risks are difficult
             to assess using currently available measurement techniques or approaches.
MEASURE 3.3: Feedback processes for end users and impacted communities to report problems
             and appeal system outcomes are established and integrated into AI system evaluation metrics.

MEASURE 4: FEEDBACK IS INCORPORATED INTO AI RISK MANAGEMENT PRACTICES

MEASURE 4.1: Measurement approaches for identifying AI risks are connected to deployment
             context(s) and informed through consultation with domain experts and other
             end users.
MEASURE 4.2: Measurement results inform iterative improvements to the AI system, including
             by informing current and future deployment decisions.

MANAGE — Prioritize and Address AI Risks

MANAGE 1: AI RISKS BASED ON ASSESSMENTS ARE PRIORITIZED, RESPONDED TO, AND MANAGED

MANAGE 1.1: A determination is made as to whether the AI system achieves its intended
            purpose and whether it causes harm.
MANAGE 1.2: Treatments, responses, and recovery plans are developed and communicated.
MANAGE 1.3: Responses to the AI risks deemed high priority, as identified by the MAP
            function, are developed, planned, and documented.
MANAGE 1.4: Negative residual risks (defined as the sum of all unmitigated risks) to
            both downstream acquirers of AI systems and end users are documented.

MANAGE 2: STRATEGIES TO MAXIMIZE BENEFITS AND MINIMIZE NEGATIVE IMPACTS

MANAGE 2.1: Resources required to manage AI risks are taken into account — along with
            viable non-AI alternatives — to reduce the magnitude or likelihood of
            potential impacts.
MANAGE 2.2: Mechanisms are in place and applied to neutralize or offset harms from
            incidents or errors.
MANAGE 2.3: Procedures are followed to respond to and recover from any incidents or
            errors.
MANAGE 2.4: Mechanisms are in place and applied to sustain the value of deployed AI
            systems throughout their lifecycle.

MANAGE 3: AI RISKS AND BENEFITS ARE DOCUMENTED

MANAGE 3.1: AI risks and benefits are documented and communicated to relevant AI actors.
MANAGE 3.2: Post-deployment AI system monitoring plans are implemented, including
            mechanisms for capturing and evaluating input from users and other relevant
            AI actors.

MANAGE 4: RISK IS MANAGED ACROSS THE ORGANIZATION

MANAGE 4.1: Post-deployment AI system monitoring plans are in place and include
            mechanisms for capturing and evaluating input from users.
MANAGE 4.2: Procedures are followed to respond to and recover from incidents and errors
            from AI system operation.
